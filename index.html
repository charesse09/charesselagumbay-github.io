* I interviewed a woman, a 2ndyear student, 20 years old, who took a course in
food technology at Bukidnon State University. But she kept her identity hidden
while I interviewed her for her privacy; she easily answered every question I
asked and was surprised because she accepted my offer to interview her even
though she was too busy.

1. Why did you choose food tech course?

Answer: Everytime I go to grocery I will found myself spending more time reading
the nutrition facts, where it made from, and the first thing I do is looking for
the expiration date. I’m always amazed of the nutritional content and ingredient
that I don’t understand but that’s not the reason why I choose FT course. As a
kid who grow up from poverty food insecurity is the one we always dealing with.
The lack of nutrition, not tasty and fresh base on our budgeting we always prefer
the cheaper one, and end up eating expired food because according to them it is
still “okay” as long as it is still crunchy it is still okay. The food insecurity
in the Philippines is the problem that I hope to be solve and it’s much better if
I contributed on it.

2. Summarize what have you learned as food tech?

Answer: What I learn in my course is not something educational but the purpose of
a FT student who are handling food soon and deal with the problem in food industry.
The common aspects we tackle is the chemistry of food, we become familiar with
microorganisms that cause spoilage, encounter equipment/technology used in laboratory
and many more. The most important thing I learn, I think, is the application of our
knowledge in real life situation such as fermenting the vegetable to preserve it
popularly known as kimchi.

3. What is your favorite food tech subject? Why?

Answer: So far, food chemistry is my favorite subject. This is where my knowledge about
food expanded since it explore the chemistry behind food. However due to complexity
of the subject, I’m more likely to be blinded that it’s my favorite chemistry we have
since first year. It is because, chemistry may be difficult, but the food chemistry
subject is the one who will make you understand what you don’t understand and slowly
accepting the fact that food like other microorganisms has scientific explanation that
need to be explore, study, understand and solve.

4. What do you find difficult in food tech course? Why?

Answer: All major subject are difficult to me like microbiology and chemistry because
I don’t like studying hat much and it’s not like you master it overnight. Studying
chemistry and microbiology is time-consuming and I don’t have time that much since
I’m working student and It’s not also helping that I’m slow learner it took time
before I understand the topic I mean 5 years or even longer. Or being food Tech student
alone is the most difficult because we handle chemicals in laboratory, and deal with
real food at the same time where everything gone wrong.

5. Will you recommend food tech course to your friend?

Answer: Yes, not because I want my friend to suffer the same difficulty as much as I
suffer but to open her eye to the significance of being a food tech student. The purpose
of a FT might change the perception of people about our course and I want to see us
someday working in the field of food industry.

Reflection


In a Food Technology course, it is clear that personal preferences affect
how people see the subject. If you love cooking and are interested in nutrition
as well as the scientific part of food preparation, this can be the most
interesting and rewarding class for you. It opens eyes to different stages
of food production among other things such as processing methods or even
preservation techniques which may come in handy at home or when working
in food industry.On the contrary, some individuals may not be so much
attracted by cooking or science hence they might not like this course.
However, what should never be forgotten about these courses is that they
usually take a wider view of what we eat and its impact on our lives; health
inclusive but also environmental and social considerations get covered too.
Therefore through taking up such units one broadens their understanding
besides discovering new areas of interest.People appreciate Food Technology
courses based on their likings and disliking towards them but still regard
it valuable because it imparts knowledge concerning consumption processes.
